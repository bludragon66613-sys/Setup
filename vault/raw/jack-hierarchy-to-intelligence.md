## Historical Context
- Roman Army invented nested hierarchy (8 -> 80 -> 480 -> 5,000) as information routing protocol
- Prussian General Staff = middle management before the term existed
- US railroads adopted military org charts (McCallum, 1850s)
- Every org experiment since (Spotify squads, Zappos Holacracy, Valve flat) reverts to hierarchy at scale
- Constraint: narrowing span of control = more layers = slower information flow

## Block's Thesis
AI can replace what hierarchy does. Companies built as an intelligence, not a hierarchy.

### Four Building Blocks
1. **Capabilities** - atomic financial primitives (payments, lending, banking, etc). Hard to acquire, network effects, regulatory moats
2. **World Model** - company world model (understands own operations) + customer world model (per-customer financial reality from honest transaction signal)
3. **Intelligence Layer** - composes capabilities into solutions for specific customers at specific moments, proactive delivery
4. **Interfaces** - delivery surfaces (Square, Cash App, etc). Important but not where value is created

### What People Do (Edge vs System)
- Intelligence lives in the system
- People are on the edge where intelligence makes contact with reality
- Humans sense what the model can't: intuition, cultural context, trust dynamics, ethical calls
- No layers of management because world model provides context

### 3 Roles Normalized
1. **ICs** - build/operate capabilities, model, intelligence, interfaces. World model provides context that managers used to provide
2. **DRIs** - own specific cross-cutting problems. Full authority to pull resources. May persist or move as needed
3. **Player-coaches** - combine building with developing people. Replace traditional manager. No status meetings — world model handles alignment
